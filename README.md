## Introduction
Since the emergence of LLM platforms such as ChatGPT, Claude, DeepSeek, Perplexity, Gemini, and others, we (Editorial Team) at the journal have been asking ourselves: How does this affect our workflow, our ethical approach, and our originality? This 2026, as a university-based journal, we have been consulting and checking with publishers such as Wiley, Elsevier, and Springer Nature in order to reach a consensus and establish common guidelines for the use of AI and generative AI in our journal, IJANR. This will be an ongoing review process and will include recommendations and protocols for authors, reviewers, associate editors, technical editors, managing editors, and the editor-in-chief.

## Definitions

Generative AI:  Group of AI algorithms and models that are capable of producing **new content**, including texts, images, videos and problem-solving strategies, with human-like creativity and adaptability (He et al., 2025)
